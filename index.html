The yield-adjusted number is a peak, not a floor. The support exists only while the 3-month farm runs; presale tokens have no vesting, so the 78% can dump from day one. The supported MC is only reachable if the big holders sit still and you exit into the window — most holders, by definition, cannot all sell at it. Treat yield-adj P/L as a best-case exit, not a settled value.
  • The structural floor is ~(curve %). Presalers fund essentially the whole raise but own only the curve share of supply; the rest is seeded into the AMM as liquidity for tokens they don't hold. So even at 100% pass-through, collective recovery caps at that share — the default 78% means a hard ~−22% ceiling on a large raise, no matter how the yield prices in.
  • Pass-through is the real lever. Yield → APR → TVL → price leaks at every step (mercenaries take the USDC and dump the token, impermanent loss, fees, arb). 100% is the theoretical ceiling; 40–70% is closer to lived behaviour. Drag the slider down and watch break-even fall back toward the spot 46.8k.
  • Entry price is collective. The 12k buys the curve once; those tokens split pro-rata across the whole raise, so a 10 USDC wallet and a 1,000 USDC wallet enter at the same price and ride the same P/L.
  • The excess is recycled, not free. The yield pot is presalers' own money above 12k, paid back out to whoever provides liquidity — themselves if they LP, outsiders if they don't. Yield captured by mercenary LPs is a straight leakage from the presale crowd.
Supported MC = launch MC + pass-through × PV(yield pot), PV via midpoint discounting over the payout period. Mechanism model for understanding structure under stated assumptions — not price prediction, not financial advice. Real secondary price is set by reflexive flows, liquidity depth, and actual demand, none of which are modeled.

APR by turnover

The same position across daily-volume-to-marketcap ratios, at current TVL, share %, and pool type. This is the curve that decays as a memecoin cools — read it top-to-bottom as the life of the token.

The APR is rented, not owned. It holds only while two things last: high turnover (which decays as attention fades) and the creator's chosen share window. When the share duration ends, total APR drops to the base-fee line — usually too thin to hold mercenary liquidity, so LPs exit and the pool thins exactly when the token is most fragile. Annualizing a launch-week turnover into a headline APR is the standard way these numbers mislead.
  • The creator fee dwarfs the LP fee. Above 59k USDC market cap the creator earns 0.95% of volume vs 0.20% to LPs — so the shared rewards, not base fees, are what make LPing worth it. Slide share to 0% to see how unattractive the base pool is alone.
  • APR scales with turnover ÷ TVL. Double the volume or halve the liquidity and APR doubles. Early thin-TVL high-volume windows print absurd APRs that are real for days and gone in weeks.
  • Impermanent loss eats directional moves. A token that 5×'s or drops 80% hands LPs meaningful IL versus just holding. Net-vs-HODL below tells you whether the fees covered it.
  • Shared rewards are funded by traders, including the arb loop. Creator fees come from volume; a chunk of that volume is canonical-vs-non-canonical arbitrage the structure itself generates. Real fees, partly self-manufactured demand.
